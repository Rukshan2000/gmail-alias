What are Gmail Aliases?

Overview

Gmail aliases are alternative email addresses you can create using your existing Gmail account. They allow you to receive emails at different addresses while using the same inbox, helping you organize, filter, and protect your primary email.

All emails sent to your aliases arrive in your main Gmail inbox, but you can easily filter and organize them using Gmail's powerful filtering system.

Benefits

  • • Organize emails by category or source
  • • Track which services share your email
  • • Protect your primary email from spam
  • • Create temporary addresses for signups
  • • No need to create multiple accounts

How It Works

  • • Gmail ignores dots in your username
  • • Plus (+) adds keywords to your address
  • • All variations point to your main inbox
  • • Use filters to automatically organize
  • • Works with any Gmail account

Types of Aliases

Plus (+) Aliases

Add keywords after a plus sign in your email address.

Original: yourname@gmail.com

Aliases:

• yourname+shopping@gmail.com

• yourname+work@gmail.com

• yourname+newsletter@gmail.com

Perfect for: Tracking signups, organizing emails, temporary uses

Dot (.) Variants

Gmail ignores dots in usernames, creating natural aliases.

Original: johnsmith@gmail.com

Aliases:

• john.smith@gmail.com

• j.ohnsmith@gmail.com

• jo.hn.smith@gmail.com

Perfect for: Different services, avoiding detection, backup addresses

Random Aliases

Combine plus notation with random words for unique addresses.

Examples:

• yourname+sky47@gmail.com

• yourname+temp-blue@gmail.com

• yourname+trial-moon@gmail.com

Perfect for: Privacy, temporary signups, one-time uses

Gmail Filter Setup

Step 1: Go to Gmail Settings → Filters and Blocked Addresses

Step 2: Click "Create a new filter"

Step 3: In "To" field, enter your alias (e.g., yourname+shopping@gmail.com)

Step 4: Choose actions: Apply label, Skip inbox, etc.

Step 5: Save filter and apply to existing emails if needed
